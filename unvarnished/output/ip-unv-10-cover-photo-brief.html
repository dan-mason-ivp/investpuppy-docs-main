Photographer Brief

UNV-10 Cover: Option A

The overcrowded abandoned boardroom table · One notepad, one pen · No people

"9 Women Cannot Make a Baby in 1 Month" is the tenth paper in the Unvarnished series — field notes from decades of bad projects. The paper argues that adding more people to a knowledge-work project does not compress the timeline, drives up coordination cost, and — when people arrive too late — actively prevents delivery by consuming the expert's capacity at the moment it is most needed. The cover should communicate this without caption or explanation.

Technical Specifications

Requirements
Format
Portrait (vertical). A4 proportions or equivalent (210 × 297mm / 8.27 × 11.69 in).
Resolution
Minimum 300dpi at final print size. Preferred: 600dpi or RAW/TIFF delivery.
Colour
Colour photograph. Slightly desaturated post-processing acceptable — the series uses muted, documentary tones. Not black and white.
Safe zone
The bottom third of the image will carry the paper title, series mark, and InvestPuppy logo. Keep this zone free of primary subject matter. The main composition should occupy the upper two-thirds.
Delivery
JPEG (high quality) and RAW or TIFF. Named: unv10_cover_photo.jpg / unv10_cover_photo.tif
Rights
Full commercial rights required. InvestPuppy to hold perpetual licence for print, digital, and promotional use without attribution requirement.

The Image

Composition

Core concept: A long boardroom table. Evidence of too many people who have recently been present — but no people visible. At the far end of the table: a single notepad, open, with handwriting visible but not readable, and a pen resting across the page. Everything else on the table suggests a crowd. The notepad suggests one person who was there to work.

The image must communicate its meaning without a caption. The contrast between the accumulated evidence of many people and the single focused object is the story.

Objects on the Table

Props and placement
ObjectQuantity and detailPlacement
Chairs 6–10 chairs. Pushed back at different angles — not neatly tucked in. People left at different times, not together. Both sides of the table, some pulled back from the table surface, one or two slightly askew.
Laptops 4–5 laptops. Some open, some partially closed. Different makes and models. One with a power cable trailing off the edge. Distributed unevenly across the table. Not arranged. Abandoned mid-session.
Coffee cups 5–7 cups. Mix of disposable takeaway cups (with sleeves) and ceramic cups. Some with remnant coffee. One with a lipstick mark on the rim. One with a pen scribbled note on the sleeve. Various positions — not clustered. One near the edge as if about to be forgotten.
Printed documents One or two small stacks of printed pages. Face down or loosely fanned. Not important enough for anyone to take with them. Mid-table. Not at the notepad end.
Water glasses / bottles 2–3 glasses at various fill levels, or branded water bottles. Scattered. One empty, tipped slightly.
Notepad The key object. A4 or legal-size notepad. Open to a page with handwriting — dense, purposeful, visible but not readable. Pen resting diagonally across the page. No laptop nearby. This position is the one place where someone was actually working. Far end of the table from camera. Isolated. A clear visual contrast with the accumulated overhead at the near end.
Name placards / tent cards Optional — adds the "project meeting" atmosphere. If used: standard white tent cards, blank or with generic role titles (PROJECT MANAGER, CHANGE LEAD, WORKSTREAM LEAD, etc.). Along the table sides. Not at the notepad position.

Lighting and Atmosphere

Mood

Time of day: After the meeting has ended. Not during. The room has been occupied. It has been vacated. The overhead fluorescent or LED panel lighting is still on — institutional, slightly harsh. Natural light from windows is acceptable as a secondary source but should not dominate.

Tone: Documentary. Not styled or aspirational. This is not a corporate brochure photograph of a beautiful boardroom. This is a photograph of what a project meeting looks like after everyone has left. The mess is deliberate and important.

Colour temperature: Slightly cool or neutral. The fluorescent-lit institutional boardroom palette. Not warm-toned or golden-hour lit.

Depth of field: Sufficient depth to keep the full table length in focus. The notepad at the far end must be identifiable as a notepad with handwriting. Shallow-focus bokeh that blurs the far end is not appropriate for this image.

Camera Position

Framing

Position: Slightly elevated — approximately standing eye level or just above. Not a bird's-eye overhead view. The camera should be at the near end of the table looking along its length toward the notepad at the far end.

Angle: Straight down the length of the table or very slightly off-axis. The full length of the table should be visible, establishing the scale and the distance between the occupied end and the working end.

Framing: The table should occupy most of the frame. The room — ceiling, walls, windows — provides context but is secondary. The viewer's eye should travel down the table from the accumulated debris to the notepad.

What to avoid
  • People in the frame — the room must be empty. The story is told by absence.
  • Branded items — no visible laptop brand logos, recognisable coffee chain cups, or company names on any printed material.
  • Aspirational or beautiful boardroom styling — this is not a prestige office photograph.
  • Extreme wide-angle distortion — the table should look like a table, not an architectural feature.
  • Soft focus on the notepad — it must be identifiable as a working document.
  • An overly tidy "staged" appearance — the mess is the point. If it looks like a photoshoot, reshoot.
  • Visible written text that is readable — handwriting on the notepad should be visible but not legible. Printed text on documents should face down or be out of focus.
  • Any InvestPuppy or Vektor branding in the scene — the cover design applies the branding; the photograph should be neutral.
Why this image

The paper describes a specific moment: additional consultants are assigned to a failing project and arrive on the morning the deliverable was due. The experienced person who has been doing the work must now bring them up to speed — eight weeks of context, decisions, and dead ends — at the moment when that time cannot be spared.

The overcrowded abandoned table communicates this story without naming it. The chairs pushed back at different angles: people arrived, occupied space, generated overhead, and left. The laptops and coffee cups: evidence of activity without evidence of output. The notepad at the far end: the one person who was there to do the work.

The image should make the viewer feel that they have been in this room. Not as a visual impression — as a physical memory. Every experienced practitioner has been in this room. The cover photograph is the moment of recognition before the first word is read.

Series Context

Reference

The Unvarnished series uses documentary-style cover photographs that communicate the paper's central argument without caption. Previous covers include: a UAT sign-off document with a packed bag visible and empty chairs behind it (UNV-09); a Swiss Army knife resting on a financial spreadsheet (UNV-07); a jammed photocopier paper tray (UNV-01). The visual language is consistent: real objects in real environments, no people, no staging, slightly institutional in tone.

The cover photograph occupies the upper two-thirds of the A4 cover page. The InvestPuppy logo, paper title, and series mark are placed over the lower third. The photograph should work as a full bleed image with this overlay in mind.
