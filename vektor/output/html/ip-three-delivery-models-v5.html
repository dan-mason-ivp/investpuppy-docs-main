Commercial Reference

One Platform.
Three Delivery Models.

A reference for commercial conversations — produced on request to explain and validate the Vektor channel architecture.

Vektor is one platform. The methodology, signal generation, ML validation layer, portfolio construction logic, and audit trail are identical in every deployment.

What differs is not the product. What differs is the commercial relationship through which it reaches different clients, and the operational scale at which it is deployed.

Three delivery models were named during the design phase — not as a commercial afterthought, but as an architectural recognition that the same product serves three genuinely different relationships in the wealth management ecosystem. The data governance framework, deployment architecture, and commercial terms for each model were developed alongside the platform — not applied retrospectively.

Why Three Delivery Models?

The architecture

The three delivery models emerged from a single observation: the buyer, the commercial relationship, the data governance requirement, the procurement process, and the support model differ materially across the three — even though the product is identical.

  • A boutique DPM or EAM subscribes directly, operates the platform for their own client mandates, and has a personal direct relationship with InvestPuppy.
  • An institutional investor — a large family office, an institutional DPM firm, a mid-size asset manager — subscribes directly at larger scale with formal governance and institutional commercial requirements. Same direct relationship, different scale and operational complexity.
  • A custodian bank or wealth platform does not subscribe to use Vektor for its own mandates. It licenses the platform, embeds it in its EAM service offering, and builds its own product on top. InvestPuppy's commercial counterpart is the bank. The EAM clients are the bank's clients.

These are three genuinely different commercial relationships requiring three clearly named delivery models.

Section 3 · Your Client Data Across the Three Models

Data residency

The most substantive functional difference between the three delivery models is what happens to your clients' identifying information — their names, addresses, and identification details. This is the data that privacy law protects, that banking secrecy obligations govern, and that institutional procurement teams ask about first.

Model What InvestPuppy holds What this means in practice When this drives model selection
Vektor : Boutique Client names and identifying information, held in InvestPuppy's cloud under a Data Processing Agreement. InvestPuppy is a data processor. A breach of InvestPuppy's cloud constitutes a personal data breach. Notification obligations apply to InvestPuppy. Appropriate where standard cloud data processing is acceptable under your regulatory framework and client governance requirements.
Vektor : House
(standard)		 Same as Boutique. Client names and identifying information in InvestPuppy's cloud under a DPA. Same breach and notification position as Boutique. Institutional DPA with stronger terms and audit rights. Appropriate for institutional clients whose data governance requirements are satisfied by a robust DPA and regional cloud deployment.
Vektor : House
(hybrid)		 Pseudonymised portfolio data only. Client names and identifying information never enter InvestPuppy's systems at any point. A breach of InvestPuppy's cloud does not constitute a personal data breach — no personal data is held there. InvestPuppy can state: "We have never seen your clients' names." The right choice when client identity must not leave your infrastructure — constitutional, regulatory, or professional requirement. Selected during scoping, not as a separate commercial tier.
Vektor : Wrapped InvestPuppy holds no EAM client data whatsoever. The bank manages all client identity. InvestPuppy's commercial relationship is with the bank, not the EAM's end clients. InvestPuppy is not a data processor for EAM end clients. No DPA between InvestPuppy and the EAM is required. The bank carries all data controller obligations for its clients. Applies by nature of the licensing model. EAMs accessing through a Wrapped bank receive the strongest structural data protection without requesting it — it is the default architecture of the channel.
Vektor : House — the hybrid deployment option explained

The hybrid deployment is not a separate tier or a premium variant of Vektor : House. It is a data governance configuration available to any House client whose requirements make it appropriate. The product is identical. The commercial relationship is the same. The difference is operational: in the hybrid configuration, an identity vault — a small, secure key-value store — is installed on the client's own infrastructure. This vault holds the only mapping between InvestPuppy's anonymous reference codes and real client identities.

InvestPuppy's cloud processes portfolio positions, orders, signals, and market data — all referenced by anonymous codes. The vault is queried only when a human needs to display a real client name, and that query happens entirely within the client's own network.

The hybrid configuration commands a higher implementation fee reflecting the vault installation and integration work, and a higher annual platform fee reflecting the ongoing operational complexity. The deployment configuration is determined during scoping, not offered as a commercial upsell.

Select the hybrid configuration when one or more of the following applies:

  • Constitutional or governance documents specify that client investment records must reside on infrastructure under the institution's direct control
  • Swiss Bankgeheimnis obligations or equivalent banking secrecy requirements demand client identities remain within the institution's infrastructure
  • Institutional procurement or compliance requires a vendor that does not hold client personal data
  • The institution requires the ability to state unqualifiedly: "No third-party system processes our clients' names"

Contact InvestPuppy for a scoping discussion to determine the appropriate configuration and applicable pricing.

Which Delivery Model Is Right for You?

Decision framework

The right delivery model is determined by the nature of your commercial relationship, your intended use of the platform, and your data governance requirements — not by your size or assets under management alone.

QuestionDirection
Do you manage portfolios directly for your own clients? Yes — continue. Wrapped if you are a bank or platform building for your clients.
Are you a practitioner-scale operation: one to a small team, boutique firm or independent practice? Boutique
Are you an institutional-scale operation: investment team, formal governance, institutional SLA and account management requirements? House — standard or hybrid configuration determined during scoping.
Do you have a requirement — constitutional, regulatory, or institutional — that your clients' names must not be held by any third-party system? House hybrid deployment, or Wrapped if accessing through your custodian bank. Contact InvestPuppy to determine which applies.
Does your custodian bank already offer Vektor through its EAM services platform? Wrapped via your bank. Or subscribe directly as Boutique or House. Contact InvestPuppy to confirm whether your custodian is in the Wrapped network.
Are you a custodian bank or wealth platform evaluating Vektor as a licensed capability for your EAM clients? Wrapped — the licensing channel.
Does your institution operate both an internal DPM function and an EAM services platform for external clients? Both House and Wrapped — one master agreement, two schedules. See the dual-channel note below.
The dual-channel institution

A single institution — for example, a custodian bank with both an internal DPM team and an EAM services platform — may operate simultaneously as a Vektor : House client and a Vektor : Wrapped licensee. This is a recognised and supported configuration governed by a single master agreement with two separate schedules, two operationally isolated Vektor deployments, and a strategic partnership recognition reflecting the depth of the combined relationship. The channel is determined by the delivery model, not the institution's identity. Contact InvestPuppy if your institution may qualify for a dual-channel arrangement.

Migration between delivery models Transition between channels is supported in both directions and is a defined, documented process. A Boutique subscriber whose custodian bank joins the Wrapped network can migrate to bank-delivered access. A Wrapped end user who changes custodian can migrate to direct Boutique or House subscription. Within the House tier, moving between standard and hybrid deployment configurations is a scoping and implementation decision, not a commercial tier change. Migration is the correct term — there is no upgrade or downgrade.
