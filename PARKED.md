## P1 — BRAND STORY DOCUMENT

**Priority:** HIGH — this is already on the outstanding items list as a stub file needing regeneration.
**Vehicle:** vk5-brand-story.docx (currently a stub in the Vektor suite)
**Format:** Vektor design system (gold accent, Helvetica, dark)

### The brief

The InvestPuppy name comes up as a concern in every review. Dan's four-point case for the name is strong and correct but currently exists only in conversation. The brand story document is the place to write it down.

**Dan's four points (write these in, in the Unvarnished voice):**
1. Makes clear — before anything else — that we are different
2. Acts as a conversation starter: raised as a question in the first 2 minutes of any meeting; opens a conversation covering brand philosophy, founder experience, and product features in a natural, lighthearted way that would never be possible in formal presentation structure
3. Reinforces that we are confident enough to operate under a lighthearted brand
4. Memorable: in a week of vendor evaluations (AlphaQuant, DeltaTrade, QuanDelta, TradeAlpha, InvestPuppy) — we are the one that sticks at the end of the week

**Five specific gaps the document should close:**

| Gap | What to include |
|-----|-----------------|
| 1. Explanation not findable | Write Dan's 4-point case in the Unvarnished voice, as confident statement of intent. First page. |
| 2. Two-brand context | "InvestPuppy is the company. Vektor is the product. In regulatory and client-facing contexts, the Vektor brand is the face. InvestPuppy is the company you trust." |
| 3. Advocate's one-liner | Provide: "They named it that deliberately — it's the firm that chose to be different before you'd read a single word. Read their risk disclosure and tell me if you still care about the name." |
| 4. Memorability claim | State explicitly: there is no other name like this in the wealthtech category. Total category uniqueness is a structural competitive advantage. |
| 5. Founding philosophy | The name decision has a story and a person behind it. Tell it. Not a full biography — a founding philosophy statement that makes the name decision part of the intellectual character of the firm. |

**Voice guidance:** Write this in the Unvarnished series voice — direct, slightly wry, completely undefensive. The name question is an uncomfortable observation that InvestPuppy can make about itself before anyone else does.

**Opening line (suggested):** "We're called InvestPuppy. That was deliberate. Here is what it tells you about how we think before we've said another word."

**Closing line:** "Serious when it matters."

**Panel quote to consider using:** *"If you're evaluating five vendors in three days — AlphaQuant, DeltaTrade, QuanDelta, TradeAlpha and InvestPuppy — which one do you remember at the end of the week?"* — Eleanor Walsh, WealthTech Weekly

## P2 — SYNTHESIS / ACTION DOCUMENT

**Priority:** MEDIUM — valuable but requires the series to have circulated first.
**Format:** Separate product. Not a tenth Unvarnished paper. Not in the Vektor suite. A standalone document.
**Suggested title:** *Before week one* or *Nine questions before you sign*
**Audience:** The commissioning audience — COO, operations director, project sponsor — someone who can still change how the engagement is structured

### The brief

The Unvarnished series is nine papers of diagnosis. The synthesis document is the action output. It converts nine failure modes into nine accountability questions — the questions a commissioning executive should be able to answer before the implementation begins.

**Structure (one question per paper):**

| Paper | Accountability question |
|-------|------------------------|
| UNV-01 | Can you describe the specific pattern of failure this implementation most resembles, and what you are doing differently? |
| UNV-02 | Who is the named individual on your side with authority to make workflow decisions without referral upward — and who is their equivalent on the vendor side? |
| UNV-03 | If multiple firms are involved, which single entity is accountable for the outcome — and what happens to their engagement if the outcome is not delivered? |
| UNV-04 | Has discovery been completed and documented before configuration begins — and is the workflow map signed off by the people who do the work, not only the people who manage it? |
| UNV-05 | What is your contract's definition of "specification" — and does it include everything demonstrated and committed in the pre-contract process? |
| UNV-06 | Have the people who will use the system in production attended the specification workshops — not their managers, them? |
| UNV-07 | For each tool or methodology being deployed, can you name the specific tasks it is suited for and the specific tasks for which it is being proposed despite not being designed for them? |
| UNV-08 | What is your post-live plan — not hypercare, but the full plan — and how long does it run? |
| UNV-09 | Are the actual users running UAT, and do they have genuine sign-off authority? |

**Design note:** This document should be visually distinct from both suites. Suggested: white background, clean typography, formatted as a literal checklist/questionnaire. The contrast with the dark Vektor system signals: this is the operational tool, not the thought leadership.

**Distribution:** Alongside the Unvarnished series as a companion piece. Could be the document tile on the /unvarnished page CTA. Free, ungated.

## P5 — TEMPORAL BRAND MANAGEMENT

**Priority:** LOW now, MEDIUM when Vektor has first clients.
**Vehicle:** Not a document — a prepared statement and internal policy.

### The brief

Isabella Chen (Partner, Catalyst Fintech Ventures) identified: the two-brand separation works now because Vektor is pre-revenue. Once Vektor has clients who are asked by their own clients "who manages your portfolio system?", the InvestPuppy name will need a consistent, prepared answer in institutional contexts.

**The question that will be asked:** "We use a platform called Vektor, built by a company called InvestPuppy." *[Pause]* "InvestPuppy?"

**Prepare now, deploy when needed:**

A one-paragraph prepared statement for clients to use when this question arises in their own institutional contexts:

> "InvestPuppy is the company name. Vektor is the platform. They named the company that way deliberately — it's the firm that signals difference before it's said a single word about the product. Once you've read their risk disclosure, their What We Are Not document, and their nine-paper thought leadership series, you'll understand the name is accurate. They're the most honest firm in this space."

**Internal policy note:** Ensure all Founding Mandate clients are briefed on this before going live. The InvestPuppy name in their own regulatory and counterparty documentation (where the Vektor brand should appear) versus in word-of-mouth referrals (where InvestPuppy is the memorable name) should be clearly understood.
